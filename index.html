Technical Specifications & Built-In Features

💰 Dynamic Money Management

SQA Pro EA automatically calculates lot sizes based on your account balance and risk percentage. Default risk is 1% per trade. As your account grows, position sizes scale proportionally. You can also set a fixed lot size if you prefer manual control. Supports micro lots (0.01) for accounts as small as $200.

🛑 Hard Stop-Loss on Every Trade

Every position opened by SQA Pro EA has a pre-calculated stop-loss placed immediately at entry. No hidden exposure. No hoping for recovery. Default SL ranges from 20-80 pips depending on pair volatility and timeframe. SL levels are dynamically calculated using ATR (Average True Range) to adapt to current market conditions.

🎯 Adaptive Take-Profit & Trailing Stop

Take-profit levels are calculated using a multi-factor model combining support/resistance zones, Fibonacci extensions, and volatility bands. Once a trade reaches a configurable profit threshold, an adaptive trailing stop activates — locking in gains while allowing profitable trades to run further.

📰 Built-In News Filter

The EA automatically avoids trading during high-impact economic news events (NFP, FOMC, CPI, ECB decisions). Configurable buffer of 15-60 minutes before and after events. This prevents being caught in volatile spikes that can trigger stop-losses on otherwise valid setups.

⏰ Session Time Filters

Control exactly when the EA trades. Default settings target London and New York sessions (highest gold/forex liquidity). Asian session trading can be enabled or disabled. You can set custom time windows down to the minute. Avoid weekend gaps with automatic Friday close feature.

📉 Daily Drawdown Protection

A configurable daily loss limit (default: 3% of account balance) acts as a circuit breaker. If the EA hits this threshold on any given day, it stops trading until the next session. This is critical for prop firm compliance where daily loss limits are typically 4-5%.

🔄 Multi-Pair Correlation Filter

When running on multiple pairs simultaneously, the EA monitors correlation between open positions. It avoids opening new trades that would create excessive exposure to a single currency (e.g., won't go long EURUSD and short USDCHF simultaneously, as both are effectively the same directional bet).

📊 Multi-Layer Entry Validation

Each trade must pass through 5 independent filters before execution: (1) Trend direction on higher timeframe, (2) Momentum confirmation via RSI/Stochastic, (3) Volatility check via ATR threshold, (4) Spread filter (rejects trades when spread is too wide), (5) Time/session validation.

📐 Spread Protection

The EA includes a maximum spread filter that prevents trade entries when broker spreads widen beyond acceptable levels (common during news, rollover, and low-liquidity periods). Default max spread: 3.0 pips for XAUUSD, 1.5 pips for major pairs. Fully configurable.

🔧 Fully Configurable Parameters

While the EA ships with optimized preset files, every parameter is accessible: risk %, lot size, SL/TP multipliers, trailing trigger, daily DD limit, session hours, spread limit, news filter window, max open trades, and more. Advanced users can fine-tune to match their specific risk tolerance and broker conditions.

What is an Expert Advisor (EA)?

An Expert Advisor is a program that runs inside MetaTrader 5 and executes trades automatically based on pre-programmed rules. It analyzes price action, indicators, and market conditions — then opens and closes positions without any manual input from you.

  • Runs 24 hours a day, 5 days a week
  • Removes emotional decision-making
  • Executes trades faster than any human
  • Follows strict risk rules every single time

🛡️ Risk Management Fundamentals

Risk management is the #1 factor that separates profitable traders from blown accounts. Here's how SQA Pro EA handles it — and what you should know.

The Golden Rules of Risk

  • Never risk more than 1-2% per trade — SQA Pro defaults to 1% risk per position
  • Always use a stop-loss — Every SQA trade has a hard SL. No exceptions.
  • Don't over-leverage — Higher leverage ≠ higher profit. It means higher risk.
  • Diversify across pairs — Run the EA on multiple pairs to spread risk
  • Never trade money you can't afford to lose — Forex is inherently risky

Understanding Drawdown

Drawdown is the peak-to-trough decline in your account balance. Even with a 93% win rate, drawdowns will occur. SQA Pro EA is designed to keep maximum drawdown under 10%.

  • Max DD <10% — Our live accounts consistently stay under this threshold
  • Daily DD limit — The EA has a configurable daily loss limit (default 3%)
  • Recovery is mathematical — A 10% loss needs ~11% gain to recover. A 50% loss needs 100%.
  • Prop firm safe — Most firms set 5-10% max DD. Our EA respects these limits.

Lot Sizing & Position Sizing

SQA Pro EA includes dynamic lot sizing that automatically adjusts position size based on your account balance and risk percentage.

  • $200 account — Typically trades 0.01 lot (micro lots)
  • $1,000 account — Typically trades 0.05 lot
  • $5,000 account — Typically trades 0.25 lot
  • $10,000+ account — Typically trades 0.50 lot or higher
  • Fixed lot mode — You can override auto-sizing and set a fixed lot manually

📖 Trading Glossary

Key terms you'll encounter when using SQA Pro EA and trading Forex.

Expert Advisor (EA)
An automated trading program that runs inside MetaTrader, executing trades based on coded rules without manual intervention.
Lot Size
The volume of a trade. 1 standard lot = 100,000 units. 0.01 lot (micro) is the smallest size most brokers offer.
Stop-Loss (SL)
A predefined price level where a losing trade is automatically closed to limit loss. Every SQA trade has a hard SL.
Take-Profit (TP)
A predefined price level where a winning trade is automatically closed to lock in profit.
Drawdown (DD)
The peak-to-trough decline in account equity. Max DD measures the largest historical drop from a peak.
Profit Factor
Gross profit divided by gross loss. A PF of 2.38 means the system makes $2.38 for every $1 lost. Above 1.5 is considered strong.
Win Rate
Percentage of trades that close in profit. SQA Pro EA maintains a 93% win rate across all supported pairs.
Spread
The difference between bid and ask price. Lower spreads mean lower trading costs. ECN brokers offer the tightest spreads.
Slippage
The difference between expected and actual execution price. Minimized by using a VPS close to broker servers.
ECN Broker
Electronic Communication Network broker — routes orders directly to liquidity providers. Best for EA trading.
VPS
Virtual Private Server — a remote computer that runs MT5 and your EA 24/7. Essential for uninterrupted trading.
Prop Firm
Proprietary trading firm that funds traders who pass evaluation challenges. SQA Pro EA is compatible with FTMO, E8, and others.
Walk-Forward Optimization
Testing method that optimizes on past data then validates on unseen future data, repeated across multiple windows. Prevents curve-fitting.
Monte Carlo Simulation
Statistical method that randomizes trade order and parameters thousands of times to stress-test a strategy's robustness.
Martingale
A dangerous strategy that doubles lot size after each loss to recover. SQA Pro EA does NOT use martingale — ever.
Grid Trading
Opening multiple positions at fixed intervals. Can lead to catastrophic losses. SQA Pro EA does NOT use grid trading.
Hedging
Opening opposing positions on the same instrument to offset risk. Allowed on MT5 but not used by SQA Pro EA's default strategy.
Trailing Stop
A dynamic stop-loss that moves in your favor as the trade profits, locking in gains. SQA Pro EA uses adaptive trailing.
